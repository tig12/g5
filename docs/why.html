Why Gauquelin5 ?

I started this software because I'd like to answer a question : Do the statistical tests done on astrology by Gauquelin and successors give significant results or not ?

A confuse situation

Reading available documentation does not permit to answer the question. The topic is highly controversial, "mars effect" polemic lasted from the 1950's to the 1990's, and still today, there is no consensus.
There are roughly three kinds of conclusions :
  • Skeptics think that Gauquelin tests have been invalidated by counter-experiences lead by different groups of skeptics.
    They do not consider Suitbert Ertel's re-analysis as significant.
  • Believers think that Gauquelin tests were reproduced and confirmed, that counter-experiences lead by skeptic groups were entached with fraud.
    They think that Ertel's introduction of citation counts to construct an objective criteria of eminence confirmed an reinforced Gauquelin claims.
  • Some people admit the existence of statistical effects, but think that they may have been generated by false declaration to civil registries.

But there must be an answer

Trying to find out if statistical effects have been detected is a different question than trying to find out if astrology is true or not.
Said an other way : observing the presence or absence of a statistical effect is different from the interpretation of this effect.

So I think that there should be a clear, "scientific" answer to my question.

Documentation and open data

The more general question about the possibility to detect traces of astrology using statistics is out of scope of this program, which deals with the historical period 1955 - 1996. As there is no consensus, current and next generations need to re-examine the whole history. This implies an easy access to data and original publications. This program is a step towards this goal.
I believe that open data and free software are necessary to do permit a re-examination of scientific value.
The hope is that free software and open data will generate a user-friendly, precise picture of this topic, to allow statisticians to examine, analyze, understand, explain and give their advices.
