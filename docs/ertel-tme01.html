TME - The Tenacious Mars Effect

Work in progress
TME is a book by Suitbert Ertel and Kenneth Irving published in 1996 (ISBN: 9781871989151).
Suitbert Ertel was a German psychologist from the university of Göttingen. This book contains an exposé of his research about the "mars effect" story, which started in the mid-1980s.
Ertel introduced eminence ranking, an "objective" way to know how famous is a person. He gathered ALL the data used in the tests related to the "mars effect" story, authored by Michel and Françoise Gauquelin and by three skeptic groups : Comité Para (Belgium 1976), CSICOP (U.S. 1979), CFEPP (France 1996).
Armed with this new tool, he revisited all the tests related to the mars effect.
His conclusion is that skeptics (at least CSICOP and CFEPP) have manipulated their data to make the effect disapear.

Such an assertion needs to be verified, and the purpose of this page is to provide the details of Ertel's arguments. As the book is only published in a paper version, these details are not present on the internet. Ertel doesn't even have a page on Wikipedia (as of 2025), despite the fact that he is one of the main protagonist of the mars effect story.

Kenneth Irving, one of the authors of this book, kindly allowed me to scan and publish some extracts of the book.
